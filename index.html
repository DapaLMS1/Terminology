The Building Blocks of Medical Language - Reference Cards

These cards break down complex medical terms. Associated terms share a color!

Prefix

Appears at the beginning, describes location, time, number, or intensity.

Hypo-

This prefix signifies a position under or below normal. An example is Hypothermia, which means below normal body temperature.

Pre-

Used to denote before in time, terms like Preoperative refer to the time period before a surgical procedure.

Peri-

This prefix indicates a position around or surrounding a structure, as seen in Pericoronitis, which is inflammation around a tooth crown.

Sub-

This term means under or below something, such as in Subgingival, which describes structures or areas located beneath the gumline.

Intra-

Used to describe a location within or inside a body cavity, for example, Intraoral refers to anything located within the mouth.

Hyper-

This prefix indicates a state of excessive or above normal activity, as found in Hyperplasia, the overgrowth of tissue.

Hypo-

Used in terms indicating below normal or deficient quantity, such as Hypocalcification, which describes insufficient calcium in the enamel.

Dys-

This prefix denotes something that is difficult or abnormal, illustrated by Dysphagia, which means difficulty swallowing.

Para-

This prefix signifies a position beside or near a structure, as in Parotid, which refers to a salivary gland region near the ear.

Root Word

The base of the term, usually referring to a body part, structure, or system.

Dent- / Odont-

This root word refers to the tooth, as seen in terms like Dentition and Orthodontics, which relate to the arrangement and alignment of teeth.

Gingiv-

This root specifically refers to the gums, and is used in terms such as Gingivitis, which means inflammation of the gums.

Enamel / Amel-

This term refers to Enamel, which is the hard outer layer of the tooth. It is used in terms like Hypo-amelogenesis (under-developed enamel).

Pulp-

This root refers to the central, soft tissue of the tooth known as the dental pulp, as in Pulpitis (inflammation of dental pulp).

Alveol-

This term refers to a socket or bone cavity, such as the Alveolar ridge, which is the bone supporting the teeth.

Or- / Stomat-

Both of these roots refer to the mouth, and are used in terms like Stomatitis, which is the inflammation of the mouth.

Maxill- / Mandibul-

These roots refer to the jaw bones. Maxillary arch refers to the upper jaw structure, while Mandibul- relates to the lower jaw.

Cephal- / Cran-

These terms refer to the head or skull, often used in diagnostic terms like Cephalometric analysis (head measurement for orthodontics).

Cardi-

This root word refers to the Heart, as found in related terms like Cardiology, which is the specialized study of the heart.

Suffix

Comes at the end, usually describes a condition, disease, or procedure.

-itis

This suffix indicates inflammation of a part, as seen in the term Gingivitis, which is inflammation of the gums.

-ectomy

This suffix denotes surgical removal of a body part, such as in Odontectomy, which is the surgical removal of a tooth.

-algia / -dynia

These suffixes are used to describe the sensation of pain, as demonstrated by Odontalgia, the medical term for a toothache.

-osis

This suffix indicates a condition, usually abnormal, such as Halitosis, which is the medical term for bad breath.

-plasty

This suffix refers to surgical repair or reshaping, for instance, Gingivoplasty is the surgical reshaping of gum tissue.

-logy / -logist

These suffixes describe a field of study or a specialist, such as Periodontologist (a specialist in gum diseases).

-gram / -graph

These terms refers to a record or the instrument used to record data, for example, a Radiograph is the image record produced by X-rays.
